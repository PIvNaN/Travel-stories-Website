Welcome to Pchelina lake

         The little church with the patron "Saint John the Baptist - Summer" is located in the former territory of the now defunct - flooded by the waters of Lake Pchelina village Pchelintsi, in "the stone", 2 km northwest of the village Potsurnentsi. The church is built on the spectacular cliffs on the south shore of Lake Pchelina (formerly carried the name Lobos).

      The church is mentioned from Konstantin Irechek, who noted that "There Pchelintsi lie on a peak ruins of a fortress and church; there are no huge pottery and Bakarena network of armor for the head of the military horse. "From the settlement and necropolis are not left visible traces only small Christian church in the former area "Bee stone."

      According to a statement of St. Mikhailov 1973 in place of fully-washed donor's inscription on the western wall of the church was later inscription, probably written in re repainting the church in which one could read the name of Saint Nicholas of Myra and the name of the donor Peyo. This unsaved inscription perhaps indicates that the original patron of the church was St. Nicholas. According to Mikhailov remains of his image was on the north side before missing iconostasis, which was the usual place for the image of the patron of the temple."
